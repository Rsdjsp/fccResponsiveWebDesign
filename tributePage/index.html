History of Formula One

  • 1906 The first and only career that the Grand Prix name was organized by the Automobile Club of France (CAF) and it ran for two days in the middle of June.
  • 1924 The Association Internationale des Automobile Clubs Reconnus (AIACR), whose mission was to regularly regulate the GG.PP. However, these regulations were abandoned in 1928, in an era known as 'Free Formula'
  • 1933 The Monaco GP was the first time in the history of the sport that the starting order was decided through qualifying times. All competing vehicles were painted in their national colors, although in 1934, the Germans stopped painting their racing cars (Auto Union and Mercedes-Benz), in an effort to reduce the weight of the car.
  • 1950 Several Grands Prix came together to create the World Championship of Formula 1 Drivers. A scoring system and agreed to a total of seven eligible races for such a celebration. The first race of the World Cup was held on May 13 at the Silverstone circuit (UK).
